ForbesThe ConversationAI Accelerator Institute
  • This is what I would refer to as the most typical use of an advertising image.
  • The article has a negative outlook of the topic and demonstrates this with a negative image (humans turning into robots)
  • This article from "The Conversation" takes a slightly different approach.
  • Despite being an outwardly negative article, the image used to market it is more serene and hopeful even.
  • I believe this image was used here because it stands out amongst it's peers and possibly even suggests that although AI could kill creativity, there is a way to prevent it, and thus acts as a call to action.
  • Again I believe this article has a different methodology behind it's advertising image as well.
  • As documented earlier in week 5, this article has an obvious bias towards AI and yet, uses a negative image here.
  • I believe this might be to bring reader's expectations down before lifting them back-up again, in a somewhat sly way to make the reader adopt the writer's viewpoint.
