Week 8: Audio & Multimedia

IL Task 1: Analyse the Use of Audio on the Web

From what I could find, the two websites that fit my theme and/or style that use audio are:

  1. Downstream
  2. Dezea

What is the audio used for?

    • Downstream's site uses audio for background ambience, button and keyboard input response and other interactables (like the minigame) on-screen.

    • Dezea similarly uses audio for button inputs but also includes sound for mousing over buttons.
    • It has something more akin to background music than the tech-y ambience of Downstream's website.

Does the audio intentionally enhance information comprehension, retention and appeal?

    • The notes that play upon pressing the buttons in the simon says minigame are a staple of the game and help players memorize the pattern.
    • The satisfying jingle that plays when you win the game also provides a sense of accomplishment.
    • The rumbling background ambience does give the site a sense of immersion and conveys the tech-y/futuristic vision it's aiming for.

    • Dezea's use of mouse-over sound effects prompts the user to click on the buttons and explore more of the site, aiding in retention.
    • Because of the sites scroll-focused browsing however there are surprisingly few oppertunities for this to come up.

Advantages or disadvantages of the audio used?

    • The feedback from the buttons definitely made me want to continue playing the minigame, without this I don't think it would be nearly as appealing.
    • The background ambience, while somewhat immersive, was overall too loud and without a way to turn it down or disable it without silencing the other sound effects, feels more intrusive than anything.

    • In a very similar sense to the first website, the mouse-over sounds on the buttons made me want to explore the website further and find other uses of this effect.
    • But also in a similar way, the inability to disable the music, which in this case was very repetitive, without disabling the sound effects made the entire implementation of sound feel wasted.

How is the audio combined with other media?

    • The button pressing audio combined with the visual pressing of the buttons.
    • Clicking the playtest report would produce a printer sound while visually displaying information.
    • Clicking the video on the right of the screen plays a static effect both auditorily and visually before swapping the video.

    • Buttons light-up and move when you mouse over them, synced to their sound effects.
